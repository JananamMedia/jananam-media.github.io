Jananam is not a media company that happens to have a pregnancy channel and a spiritual podcast. It is something more specific — a house of conscious content, built around the passages that actually change a human life.

SuperMummy was born from a simple observation: Indian women navigating their first pregnancy deserved content in their own language, medically sound, made with genuine care — not repurposed from Western sources, not patronising, not cluttered with anxiety. Seven years and eighty-three million views later, it is the channel many of them found when they needed it most.

The podcast and the book came from a different kind of longing — the longing of someone who spent years studying the MahaShivPuran and wanted to share what she found. Not as performance. Not as content strategy. As seva. The same impulse, a different passage.

These things belong together because they are made from the same place: the conviction that people deserve content that takes their transformation seriously. That was the beginning. That is still what we make.
